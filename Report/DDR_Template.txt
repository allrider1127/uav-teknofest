AND OPERATION COMPETITION DETAILED EVALUATION
REPORT PREPARATION GUIDELINE
This guide serves as a guide for the Detailed Evaluation Report (DDR) that teams applying to
the Advanced Autonomous Systems Design and Operation Competition are obliged to prepare.
It is expected that this report, which is the second milestone of the competition schedule, will
be prepared with utmost diligence based on the guidelines and technical criteria presented under
the

relevant

topic

headings.

The Detailed Evaluation Report (DDR) will be evaluated according to the table given below in
line with the criteria determined by a board possessing technical competence. Successfully
completing this stage constitutes a prerequisite for proceeding to the next technical phase.

Section 1. Summary (10 POINTS)
Within the scope of this report, a systematic and holistic summary defining the technical
outlines and operational capability of the project is required to be provided. The preliminary
design activities and original system architectures carried out by taking into consideration the
project requirements of the UAV, UGV, and Catapult systems should be defined. By evaluating
the configurations belonging to the systems with appropriate evaluation metrics, their technical
compatibility with mission scenarios, their endurance under boundary conditions, and the
mission flexibility they provide against variable operational conditions should be analyzed, and
accordingly, which of the configurations stands out should be defined together with its
justifications. Finally, how each of the systems is integrated into the mission definitions within
the scope of the competition and the contribution of these systems to operational success should
be summarized.

Section 2. Organizational Structure and Project Schedule (10 POINTS)
In order to clarify the institutional functioning and implementation phases of the program, this
section examines the organizational hierarchy in which roles are defined and the project
schedule covering the stages of the design cycle. These two fundamental elements, which
ensure that the engineering cycle progresses in a systematic order, are presented in detail under
two separate headings below.
Section 2.1. Organizational Structure; The current organizational structure of the team and
the execution authorities of the members/units should be schematized in this section. In
accordance with the objective evaluation criteria of the report, a role-based and professional
description that is completely free of personal data of the members (name, image, etc.) should
be preferred.
Section 2.2. Project Schedule; Within the scope of this section, compliance with the schedule
plan included in the project schedule previously prepared in the Preliminary Evaluation Report
should be confirmed, and if there are new workflows added to the work schedule, the planning
should be re-evaluated and updated with an analytical approach.
Section 3. General System Specifications (60 POINTS)
In this section, the system specifications defined for the main system and subsystems to be
developed within the scope of the competition must be presented in a comprehensive manner.
The aforementioned specifications will be examined within the axes of Flight Sciences,
Structural Design and Analysis, and Avionics-Software disciplines, and the system’s holistic
architecture will be addressed under three main headings.
Section 3.1. Flight Sciences - UAV Conceptual Design Principles: Under this subsection, the
technical specifications of the unmanned aerial vehicle to be designed, including weightbalance data, operational mission profile, aerodynamic structural design processes, stability
analyses, and flight performance parameters, shall be addressed in a comprehensive manner.
Section 3.1.1 Weight & Balance: In this section, the weight and balance calculations of the
unmanned aerial vehicle designed must be explained in all details under the following three
headings at the required level of detail.

Weight Estimations: The methods followed in estimating the aircraft weight, the
statistical data or empirical formulas used, and the reference points must be explained
together with their bases. Any assumptions made and safety margins, if any, must be
presented with justification.
Weight and Balance Tables: A detailed component-based mass breakdown table must
be created. The location of the center of gravity (CG) on the aircraft must be visualized.
Any assumptions or estimations made, if any, must be explained together with their
bases.
Final Moment of Inertia Values: The moment of inertia values that will serve as input
to dynamic stability analyses must be presented together with the calculation methods.
Any assumptions or estimations made, if any, must be explained together with their
bases.
Section 3.1.2 Mission Profile: The execution of the task defined within the framework of the
competition rules shall be divided into flight phases, and the mission profile of the aircraft shall
be formed and explained in detail.
Section 3.1.3 Constraint Diagram: The constraint diagram of the designed aircraft must be
explained and presented in full detail. The methodology followed while creating the constraint
diagram must be explained comprehensively. Finally, the selected design point from the
constraint diagram must be determined, and the selected values of wing loading (W/S), power
loading (W/P), and power-to-weight ratio (P/W) must be presented together with their
justification.
Section 3.1.4 Wing Design: Under this heading, the methodology followed in wing design
must be explained in full detail. All equations used or derived in the application of the
methodology must be provided. Input parameters used in the design must be presented together
with their estimated bases. The selected airfoil, wing placement, chord lengths, wingspan, and
all other parameters of the wing must be specified.
Section 3.1.5 Fuselage Design: The methodology followed in the fuselage design of the aircraft
must be explained in full detail. All equations used or derived in the application of the
methodology must be provided. Input parameters used in the design must be presented together

characteristics of the designed aircraft must be explained in full detail. The methods and tools
used in the stability analyses of the aircraft (if any) must be presented comprehensively.
Section 3.1.10 Flight Envelope: The flight envelope of the aircraft must be drawn in
accordance with the constraints specified in the requirements, including gust lines. The gust
equations from CS-VLA must be used.
Section 3.1.11 Performance Calculations: The performance calculations for the aircraft must
be explained in full detail. The methods (analytical or numerical) and tools (if any) used in the
performance calculations must be presented comprehensively.

Section 3.2 Structural Design and Structural Analysis
In this section, the structural design parameters of the Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV),
Unmanned Ground Vehicle (UGV), and catapult systems to be developed within the scope of
the competition, as well as the structural analysis studies conducted to verify these designs,
must be explained in a manner that satisfies the defined technical requirements.
Section 3.2.1 Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV): Under this heading, the technical
specifications of the platform must be addressed in terms of structural design and structural
analysis in a complementary manner. Structural requirements from the provided requirements
list should be used as a basis, and if necessary, new requirements may be derived; all
requirements defined for this stage must be satisfied. Requirement traceability must be
considered, and appropriate verification methods (analysis, test, inspection, similarity, etc.)
must be defined.
Within the scope of Structural Design processes:
• The master geometry must be fully designed,
• Preliminary design of all components must be completed,
• Control surfaces must be added,
• Necessary interfaces for motor integration must be defined,
• Placement of all systems within the aircraft must be completed,
• A detailed mass distribution table for the current stage of the aircraft must be prepared,
• The center of gravity location (x, y, z coordinates) must be shown using three-dimensional
data,
• All fasteners planned to be used in the aircraft must be defined,
• The assembly sequence of the aircraft must be explained within the concept of modularity,
• A part numbering and revision system must be established,
• A Bill of Materials (BOM) must be prepared.
Within the scope of structural analysis processes:
• All calculations to be performed within structural analysis must be demonstrated either
through basic (hand calculations) or finite element method approaches,

Section 3.3 Avionics & Software
In this section, the system architecture, mission flowcharts, hardware selection, wiring
activities, EMI/EMC measures, power systems, and battery calculations of the Unmanned
Aerial Vehicle (UAV) and Unmanned Ground Vehicle (UGV) systems to be developed within
the scope of the competition, as well as the details of the planned software activities, must be
included.
Section 3.3.1 System Architecture and Mission Flowcharts: For both the UAV and UGV
separately, detailed and readable connection diagrams must be provided, showing how all
electronic components are interconnected and distinguishing signal lines (PWM, UART, I2C,
etc.) and power lines using color coding. In addition, detailed mission flowcharts for both the
UAV and UGV must be provided. Finally, logic diagrams of fail-safe scenarios created in
accordance with the requirements must be presented within this section.
Section 3.3.2 Hardware Selection and Comparative Analysis: The selection process of all
avionics components used in the UAV and UGV platforms must be presented in detail, based
on comparative analyses with market alternatives, along with technical justifications and
performance advantages of the selected products. In addition, comprehensive Bills of Materials
(BOM) for UAV and UGV platforms must be presented separately in table format, including
the name, brand/model information, quantity, unit and total weight, physical dimensions, and
estimated cost of all electronic components.

Section 3.3.3 Electrical Design and Cabling: The selection of cable cross-sections (AWG)
based on current-carrying capacity must be demonstrated with calculations. Connector types
(XT60, Molex, JST, etc.) selected considering vibration and environmental factors, as well as
locking mechanisms, must be specified. Cable weight calculations must be performed, and their
effect on the total weight budget must be shown. In addition, EMI/EMC design measures taken
to prevent electromagnetic interference must be explained.
Section 3.3.4 Power System Analysis and Battery Calculations: For both UAV and UGV
separately, a power consumption table including the average and peak current values of all
consumer components in the system must be created. In addition, battery capacity (mAh)
calculation methods must be provided in detail, considering mission duration (flight/driving
time) and safety margins. The suitability of the battery discharge rate (C rating) must be
mathematically demonstrated based on the instantaneous maximum current drawn from the
system.
Section 3.3.5 Software Architecture and Image Processing: This section may be examined
under three subheadings:
Software Infrastructure: The programming languages (Python, C++, etc.), libraries (OpenCV,
ROS, TensorFlow, etc.), and operating systems selected for autonomous systems on the UAV
and UGV must be explained together with the reasons for selection and their advantages.
Image Processing Algorithm: The working principle of the image processing or artificial
intelligence (AI/ML) model used by the UAV for route detection, including dataset preparation,
training process, and performance metrics, must be explained and supported with block
diagrams.
Communication Protocol: The data communication architecture between the UAV and UGV
platforms, and the protocols implemented to ensure data integrity and security, must be
explained in technical detail.

and verification activity must be clearly defined. The content presented under this heading is

Section 5. Requirement Traceability and Evaluation (10 POINTS)
In this section, teams are expected to systematically present how the requirements listed in the
competition requirement set have been addressed throughout the system development process,
to what extent they have been fulfilled, and by which verification methods they are managed.
Teams must present the distribution of satisfied, under-verification, and unfulfilled
requirements using technical data, clearly specify the verification method used for each
requirement, and support this information with visual tools such as tables, traceability matrices,
graphs, or pie charts. Teams are required to base their verification approach on the Means of
Compliance (MoC) methodology. Accordingly, the verification method for each requirement
(analysis, test, inspection, demonstration, etc.) must be defined, and the relationship between
verification activities and system requirements must be clearly shown. For unmet requirements,
the planned verification method, completion approach, and targeted verification process must
be specified. The content under this section is expected to demonstrate that teams address
requirements management, traceability, and verification processes using a systems engineering
approach.
