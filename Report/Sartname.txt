1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
The Advanced Autonomous Systems Design and Operation Competition, organized by
Turkish Aerospace Industries (TUSAŞ), aims to guide young talents toward the future of
aerospace and space technologies in line with our country’s vision of a fully independent
defense industry. The competition invites associate, undergraduate, and graduate-level
students to become an integral part of our defense industry, while providing them with the
opportunity to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems and transform their
ideas into prototypes.
It is aimed that the younger generations, who will shape the future of the defense industry,
enhance their engineering capabilities, gain experience in complex system design, and
acquire the competence to contribute to global advancements in UAV technologies. This
initiative is committed to enabling young participants to take an active role in Türkiye’s
technology initiative and to positioning our country as a center of excellence in this field.
The competitions are organized under the coordination of TUSAŞ within the scope of
TEKNOFEST, with the vision of expanding and strengthening the national aerospace
ecosystem. The definition of technical rules, supervision of design processes, and
performance review are carried out by the Advisory and Review Board (ARB), consisting
of expert engineers from TUSAŞ.
This document covers all technical requirements, terms of participation, and the ethical,
safety, and technical guidelines to be followed by all participants in the competition.

2. COMPETITION DESCRIBTION
Within the scope of the Advanced Autonomous Systems Design and Operation
Competition, teams are expected to autonomously solve a logistics/transportation
problem in a simulated post-disaster scenario, through the coordinated operation of an
Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) and an Unmanned Ground Vehicle (UGV). The execution
of the mission shall be carried out in accordance with the rules and workflow specified
below.

2.1. Competition Area and Operational Environment
The defined zones specified below shall be established within the competition area by
the technical teams.
•
•

Target Detection Zone (TDZ): A 50 m × 50 m bounded area within the competition field.
This zone contains debris piles and obstacles simulating a disaster environment.
UAV Launch Zone (UAV-LZ): The area where the UAV catapult system will be installed
and from which the UAV will be launched.

7

•

•
•
•
•

UAV Operation Zone (UAV-OZ): The maximum flight area within the competition field
in which the UAV is permitted to operate. This area is limited to dimensions of 300 m
× 300 m.
UGV Positioning Zone (UGV-PZ): The area where the ground vehicle takes its initial
position. It is limited to dimensions of 1 m × 1 m.
Target Zone (TZ): The destination point that the UGV is expected to reach within the
target detection zone. It is a 2 m × 2 m area marked with a red “H” symbol.
UAV Landing Zone (UAV-LZ): The designated safe area where the UAV will land via
parachute after completing its mission. It is a 30 m × 30 m blue-colored zone.
Routes: Within the TDZ, there are three (3) different paths formed between the UGVPZ and the TZ among the debris piles. On competition days, only one (1) of these three
routes will remain open, while the other two will be physically blocked. The
information regarding which route is open will not be shared with the participants in
advance and will be kept confidential.

2.2. Mission Workflow
For the mission to be considered successful, the system must execute the following steps
sequentially and in compliance with the specified rules:

2.2.1. Takeoff
•

•

As specified in the technical requirements, all relevant systems must be transported
to the competition area in sealed containers. The setup period shall commence
following the opening of these seals under the supervision of the referees.
The UAV shall be launched only from the designated UAV Launch Zone (UAV-LZ) using
a catapult system.

8

•

Hand launch, running takeoff from a runway, or vertical takeoff methods are
prohibited; takeoff must be executed exclusively via the catapult system.

2.2.2. Reconnaissance and Route Identification:
•
•

After launch, the UAV shall switch to fully autonomous flight mode and operate
within the UAV-OZ.
Using its onboard sensors and image processing algorithms, the UAV shall identify the
open route within the TDZ.

2.2.3. Data Transmission:
•
•
•
•

The UAV shall transmit the identified route information to the ground station and the
UGV via wireless communication systems.
The field referee at the ground station must confirm in real time that the route
information has been successfully detected.
The field referee at the ground station shall also confirm that the detected route
information has been transmitted to the UGV.
This transmission must be completed while the UAV is airborne.

2.2.4 Landing
•
•
•
•

After the route information has been transmitted to the UGV, the UAV shall initiate its
landing procedure within the designated UAV-LZ.
Landing must be performed using a parachute system.
The parachute deployment command may be issued either autonomously by the
system or manually triggered from the ground station as a safety precaution.
If the parachute deployment system is triggered manually instead of autonomously, a
deduction score shall be applied to the relevant team.

2.2.5. Ground Operation
•
•
•

•
•

The UGV shall wait for the UAV to complete its landing before initiating movement. The
UGV shall not move while the UAV is still in the air.
Teams whose UGVs begin movement before the UAV has landed shall be deemed
unsuccessful in the mission.
Following the completion of the UAV landing operation, the UGV’s deployment
process shall be manually triggered by a team member at the Ground Control Station
(GCS).
Based on the route information received from the UAV, the UGV shall autonomously
navigate true the open route and proceed toward the TZ.
Any UGV that does not operate autonomously shall be deemed unsuccessful in the
mission.

9

2.2.6. Mission Completion
•
•
•

The mission shall be considered successfully completed when the UGV reaches
within the boundaries of the TZ by following the route transmited by the UAV.
Teams that fail to reach the TZ within the specified time limit shall be deemed
unsuccessful in the mission.
As defined in the technical requirements list, the UGV must transport the designated
payload(s) to the target area. Upon arrival at the target point, the UGV must maintain
its structural integrity and retain the payload(s) without any loss; otherwise, the
mission shall be deemed unsuccessful.

5.3.2. Drone Mission Test Flight
This phase is a mandatory flight phase in which the mission software and autonomy algorithms
developed by the teams for the competition are tested under field conditions. Teams are
required to perform this mission using a multirotor unmanned aerial vehicle (Drone) capable of
vertical take-off and landing, integrated with their uniqe hardware and software architectures.
The mission shall be conducted within a simulated 20×20 meter test area containing obstacles
and debris structures. Within the scope of the mission, the drone is required to detect the “Open
Path” that provides a safe transition between the designated “Start Zone” and “Target Zone” by
flying at a minimum altitude of 30 meters. This detection shall be performed using aerial image
processing and artificial intelligence algorithms, and the identified path shall be transmitted in
real time to the Ground Control Station (GCS) during flight. In order for the mission test flight to
be considered successful and for teams to receive the full score of 100 points, the real-time
path detection must be verified and approved by the field referee, and the entire operation
process (take-off, detection, data transmission, and landing) must be completed within a
maximum of 10 minutes. Teams that fail to meet any of the specified criteria, exceed the time
limit, or perform incorrect path detection shall receive a score of 0 and will be considered
unsuccessful in the mission. The total score for the final review phase is calculated according
to the following weighted average formula:
Final Review Stage Total Score = (FRR * 0.85) + (Drone Mission Test Flight * 0.15)
Based on the results of the Final Review Stage (FRS), teams that are deemed successful
shall be entitled to receive 75% of the total budget, provided that they have submitted a
financial support application. Within this scope, the financial support form to be
submitted through the KYS system must be completed in full.
The deadline for the financial support form shall be determined by the TEKNOFEST
Competitions Committee, and the relevant information shall be communicated to the
designated contact person of the respective team.
Note: Submitting a financial support application is not mandatory.

5.4. System Review Presentation (SRP):
System Review Presentation is a mandatory presentation document to be prepared by
teams for the purpose of verifiably validating the design/manufacturing/procurement
maturity of the UAV, UGV, and Catapult systems developed within the scope of the
competition. The BOM (Bill of Materials); which is the basis of the presentation, is the
official materials list that includes all manufactured or purchased parts, subsystems, and
consumables of the system (e.g., adhesives/binders), listing them with their names,
quantities, and basic information. The presentation shall include, for each part and/or
commercially procured system item included in the BOM, the manufacturing method,

18

manufacturing duration, CAD model mass, actual measured mass after manufacturing
or delivery, any additional fixture/tool requirements for manufacturing, and any
manufacturing difficulties encountered; for commercially procured subsystems,
procurement status (received / ordered / no action taken yet) along with the mass and
dimensional information of the purchased items shall also be reported. In this way, the
entirety of the declared BOM shall be checked on a part/system basis, and progress
tracking shall be standardized. No additional scoring shall be conducted after the
presentation, and the evaluation shall be announced as “Successful / Unsuccessful.”

5.5. Flight Readiness Review Report (FRRR):
Within the scope of this report, competitors shall present in detail the final images,
physical characteristics (such as dimensions, weight, etc.), and total budget analyses of
the assembled UAV and UGV systems. Differences between the actual project schedule
and the project schedule projected in the FRR shall be provided with detailed
explanations. Any changes applied to the assembly and integration steps planned in the
FRR shall be explained along with their justifications.
Competitors shall also include, within the report, videos demonstrating the successful

6. COMPETITION IMPLEMENTATION PRINCIPLES
•

•

•

Within the scope of the Advanced Autonomous Systems Design and Operation
Competition, it is mandatory that the aerial vehicle platforms to be developed are
configured as fixed-wing unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs).
The term “system” used throughout all guideline documents collectively refers to
Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), Unmanned Ground Vehicles (UGVs), and launch
(catapult) platforms in an integrated manner.
It is essential that all design, sizing, and manufacturing processes of the systems
submitted to the competition are carried out entirely by the competing teams. Teams

•

•

•

participating with commercially available off-the-shelf platforms or designs found to
contain plagiarism/copying shall be disqualified from the competition.
The maximum weight limit defined for aerial vehicles to be produced within the scope
of the Advanced Autonomous Systems Competition is 25 kg, including operational
payloads. Vehicles exceeding this limit shall be excluded from evaluation during the
technical inspection phase.
The maximum weight limit defined for ground vehicles to be produced within the
scope of the Advanced Autonomous Systems Competition is 10 kg, excluding
operational payloads. Vehicles exceeding this limit shall be excluded from evaluation
during the technical inspection phase.
The dimensions of the operational flight area and the field configuration shall be
announced to all teams through official channels after the competition venue is
determined.
The relevant institution reserves the right to make changes, updates, or revisions to
this specification and all related technical documents throughout the competition
process in accordance with operational requirements, technical needs, or force
majeure conditions. Any such changes shall become binding for all participating
teams from the moment they are announced via the official website and
communication channels of the competition.
The competition shall last a total of 6 (six) days. This 6-day period shall be divided into
3 (three) separate task periods, each consisting of two (2) consecutive days.
Within the target detection area, there are 3 (three) different routes that provide
passage between the UGV Positioning Zone (UGV-PZ) and the Target Zone (TZ). These
routes are designed in three different difficulty categories: “Easy,” “Medium,” and
“Hard.” Each route shall have a different difficulty coefficient in evaluation
calculations.
In each two-day period of the competition, only one (1) of these three routes shall be
available for use, while the other two routes shall be completely closed with physical
obstacles (barriers, debris simulations, etc.). Information regarding which route and
difficulty level will be open on which competition days shall not be shared with
competitors in advance and shall remain confidential.
Due to the uncertainty of the open route, teams are required to use sensors and image
processing technologies during the UAV’s autonomous flight to scan physical
obstacles in the field and determine the accessible route for the UGV to proceed.
Each competitor team shall have only one (1) flight attempt for each route made
available (each 2-day period). Accordingly, teams shall have a total of three (3) flight
attempts over the 6-day competition period.
The flight attempts available to teams shall only be valid within the period of the
currently open route. Any unused flight attempt within the designated period of a given
route shall not be carried over to subsequent routes or other days under any

circumstances. Unused flight attempts shall be forfeited, and the team shall receive
a score of zero (0) for the mission of that round.

6.1. Before the Competition

Competitor teams are required to complete their on-site registration procedures on
the first day of the event week. The Team Captain must be personally present during
registration, and the registration procedures shall be carried out by the Team Captain
at the Management Office. In the event that team members who are not present during
the registration hours arrive at the field later, the Team Captain, together with the
respective member, is obligated to apply to the Management Office to report the
situation and update the final registration form.
Teams that have completed on-site registration shall apply to the Technical Inspection
Tent to obtain technical compliance approval.
All teams that have completed the registration process are required to undergo the
technical inspection process prior to proceeding to the flight phase. No team that has
not completed the technical inspection phase shall be permitted to conduct flight
operations.
All decisions made by TUSAŞ officials and the jury panel during the technical
inspection phase are final and binding. Participating teams are required to comply
with these decisions unconditionally throughout the inspection process.
It is essential that teams undergo technical inspection with the system configurations
they have declared and committed to within the scope of the Final Evaluation
Presentation, System Review Presentation, and Flight Readiness Assessment Report.
Deviations from the reported technical specifications or participation with a different
system shall be considered a violation of the rules.
The storage cases of UAV and UGV systems that successfully pass technical
inspection and receive “Flight Approved” status shall be sealed by the referees. Once
sealed, any modification, disassembly, or part replacement in the mechanical or
electronic structure of the systems prior to the mission is strictly prohibited for safety
reasons.
Teams whose storage cases are sealed following technical inspection shall not
interfere with these seals until their assigned flight sequence. The opening of the seals
shall be carried out at the flight area and immediately prior to the start of the
preparation period, under the supervision of the jury panel.

Competitive teams have the right to determine their flight order based on the ranking
resulting from the Final Evaluation Presentations. The team that achieves the highest
score in the evaluation will have priority in selecting its position in the flight schedule.

No objections regarding grouping or flight order will be accepted. The established

All equipment, tools, and materials required for the execution of tasks during the
competition are the responsibility of the teams.
The responsibility for ensuring that all systems and supporting infrastructure
components to be used in operational processes are available on-site lies with the
teams.
In all possible risk scenarios, the jury has the authority to terminate the operation in
order to maintain flight safety. In the event that the UAV sustains any damage or failure
prior to or during flight, the system must undergo a comprehensive technical
inspection. Platforms that do not receive technical compliance approval as a result of
this inspection will not be permitted to continue flight operations under any
circumstances.
No appeal mechanism shall be operated for flight operations that are rejected or
terminated on safety grounds. Administrative and technical decisions taken with a
safety-oriented approach are binding for all participants; any applications made in
this regard shall be deemed invalid.
A maximum of eight (8) team members from each team are allowed to enter the
competition area.
Advisors (Academic Advisors or Mentor Teachers) are strictly prohibited from entering
the flight area.
After entering the competition area, teams are not permitted to communicate with
their advisors outside the area by verbal, telephone, or any form of signaling. If
detected, the team will be disqualified.
All team members present in the area during flight are required to wear the provided
helmets and vests.
Teams enter the competition area upon the approval of the “Area Referee.” With this
approval, the team’s 15-minute “Preparation Time” begins.
During the preparation time, teams must complete the following: UAV assembly,
placement of the catapult system inside the UAV launch zone (UAV-LZ), mounting of
the UAV onto the catapult, placement of the unmanned ground vehicle (UGV) into the
UGV positioning zone (UGV-PZ), and setup of the Ground Control Station.
The team captain declares “Team Ready” to the referee upon completion of
preparations, at which point the preparation time is stopped.
After the “Team Ready” declaration, any physical intervention on the vehicles or the
ground station by participants is prohibited.
In case intervention on the systems is required due to a technical malfunction, the
team captain may proceed by declaring to the referee that they accept a 100-point

•

penalty. The time spent for intervention is added to the team’s preparation time. If the
total allotted time is exceeded, the flight right is revoked.
After preparations are completed, “Flight Readiness” approval must be obtained from
the Area Referees. No launch may be conducted without this approval.
The flight time begins when the UAV separates from the catapult system (launch) and
ends when route determination, data transmission, and parachute landing are
completed. The maximum time allowed for these operations is 15 minutes.
After launch via the catapult system, if the UAV fails to remain airborne and contacts
the ground; provided that the Area Referee confirms that no incident or structural
damage compromising flight safety has occurred, the team is granted a re-launch
right.
The time spent for the re-launch process (retrieving the vehicle, re-installing it on the
catapult, and launching again) is not paused and is included within the total flight time
(15 minutes). Teams must complete this process within their remaining task time. No
launch may be performed once the time has expired.
During re-setup, only catapult installation and system restart procedures are
permitted. Comprehensive repairs or component replacement on the vehicle are
strictly prohibited during this process.
The open route detected by the UAV must be transmitted in real time to the Ground
Control Station and monitored by the Area Referee via the screen. The referee must
verbally confirm that the detection has been successful. Any detections not approved
by the referee shall be deemed invalid.
The UAV must conduct its flight within the defined UAV operational boundaries (UAVOB). Flights exceeding the UAV-OB limits shall be considered a “Boundary Violation,”
and the flight will be deemed unsuccessful.
The unmanned ground vehicle (UGV) may move toward the Target Zone (TZ) only after
a successful UAV mission (i.e., transmission of route data and completion of the UAV’s
landing), based on the data received from the UAV. The maximum time allowed for the
UGV to reach the TZ is 10 minutes. If this time is exceeded, the UGV task will be
considered unsuccessful.
Both the UAV and the UGV must perform all processes—from launch/start to mission
completion (including navigation, detection, data transmission, and driving)—fully
autonomously.

Upon completion of flight operations, all teams are obliged to promptly remove their
systems and equipment from the flight area. Participants are responsible for the swift
collection of equipment to ensure the field is prepared for the next flight.

6.4. Conditions for Flight to be Deemed Invalid
Mission flights performed during the competition shall be deemed “Invalid,” and no points
shall be awarded for the relevant flight round if any of the following conditions occur:
•

•

•

•

•

•

Time Exceedance: The UAV mission time begins when the UAV separates from the
catapult system and ends upon completion of route determination, data
transmission, and parachute landing. The maximum time allowed for these processes
is 15 minutes. If this duration is exceeded, the flight shall be deemed invalid even if
the mission is completed.
Manual Intervention: If, at any stage of the flight (takeoff, cruise, or landing), the Safety
Pilot intervenes and switches the UAV to manual mode due to safety concerns or a
technical malfunction, the flight shall be considered unsuccessful.
Referee Verification and Data Transmission: The route detected by the UAV must be
transmitted in real time to the Ground Control Station. If the transmitted data cannot
be verified by the Area Referee, or if data transmission is not maintained, the flight
shall be deemed invalid.
Boundary Violation: If the UAV exits the UAV operational boundaries (UAV-OB) defined
by the competition committee, the flight shall be deemed unsuccessful in
accordance with safety procedures.
Crash and Structural Integrity: If the UAV sustains a crash, breaks apart, or loses its
structural integrity at any stage of the flight or during landing (except for propeller
breakage), the flight shall be deemed unsuccessful.
Landing Violation: If, during the UAV’s landing within the designated UAV Landing Zone
(UAV-LZ) after mission completion, the parachute system fails to deploy, resulting in a
hard landing (uncontrolled descent), the flight shall be deemed invalid.
