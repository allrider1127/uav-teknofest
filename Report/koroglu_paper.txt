Optimization of an Airfoil Characteristics to Minimize the Turn Radius of a Small
Unmanned Aerial Vehicle

Suleyman Murat Koroglu, Ibrahim Ozkol
Istanbul Technical University
Faculty of Aeronautics and Astronautics
Istanbul, Turkey
e-mail: koroglu17@itu.edu.tr; ozkol@itu.edu.tr

Abstract—Recently, one of the priorities of the general aviation
all around the world is to reduce the environmental hazards of
aviation. Optimization is the endpoint to be done in order to
diminish the difference between energy used and energy
supplied for aircraft. These ideas bring some new methods and
tools to our mind for aerodynamic shape optimization should
be developed and allowing access to aircraft configuration that
could not be obtained with ancient techniques. This paper has
mainly focused on an application of optimization tool that can
reach the advanced aircraft design parameters. This research
targets to optimizing an airfoil by changing the parameters
such as maximum thickness, camber, camber location, the
angle of attack, and the wing taper ratio in order to minimize
the turn radius of a small unmanned aerial vehicle at low
Reynolds numbers. Firstly, the base airfoil, which is
NACA4412, is generated by using MATLAB [1] and it is
analyzed in XFOIL. Then, the aerodynamic characteristics of
the airfoil are evaluated thanks to MATLAB-XFOIL interface.
Eventually, the airfoil is optimized by using MATLAB fmincon
solvers, which are Sequential Quadratic Programming and
Interior-Point Methods. The results - coming from solvers - are
compared. During the iterative process, the lift coefficient and
the moment coefficient of the airfoil are kept constant. The
drag coefficient of the airfoil and the induced drag factor are
minimized to minimize the turn radius. This work in this paper
may have a certain significance for designing an optimum
airfoil in order to minimize the turn radius of an unmanned
aerial vehicle.

Airfoil design for unmanned aerial vehicles is an
essential part of UAV development process. The airfoils
currently used are pretty old like the NACA family, which
was originally developed for airplanes during World War II.
However, today, in order to obtain an optimum airfoil shape
satisfying the mentioned requirements, several techniques
have been applied during the designing sequences, through
numerical optimization [2].
The objective of the present work is to automatically
generate airfoil profiles in MATLAB by using the analytical
formula and evaluate their aerodynamic performances via
XFOIL, which is an interactive program for the design and
analysis of subsonic isolated airfoils. After having various
profiles with variable performance the optimum one among
which has been selected by using MATLAB fmincon
function, which is one of the numerical optimization tools.
fmincon attempts to find a constrained minimum of a scalar
function of several variables starting at an initial estimate
and it uses several solvers such as Sequential Quadratic
Programming, Active Set, and Interior Point. In this study,
the problem is solved by using Sequential Quadratic
Programming and Interior Point Methods the results obtained
are compared.

The optimization problem is solved by using MATLABXFOIL interface. Firstly, the airfoil is generated via
MATLAB. Then, the airfoil is analyzed by using XFOIL at

process continues until the possible minimum value of the
objective function is obtained. During the process, the lift
coefficient of the airfoil (ܿ௟ ) and the moment coefficient of

2) Genetic algorithm
For the second optimization section a single-objective
multi-nonlinear and linear constraints genetic algorithm is
applied with the help of MATLAB interface. In genetic
algorithm it is used population-based method like there is a
gene-pool that continuously regenerated and refreshed with
new generation [8]. There are two parents and randomly they
are selected. Offspring is created from them and it is
multiplied with cross-over and mutation operator. After that
the performance of the new offspring is evaluated with the
help of XFOIL. At the end of the iteration there are N
survivals and they are the best gene-type.

CONCLUSION

In this paper, NACA4412 airfoil is optimized in order to
minimize the turn radius of a small unmanned air vehicle.
First of all, the airfoil is generated in MATLAB by using
NACA 4-digit airfoil analytical formula. The airfoil analyzed
by using XFOIL. The aerodynamic properties of the airfoil
such as lift coefficient, moment coefficient, and the drag
coefficient are evaluated by using MATLAB-XFOIL
interface. The airfoil maximum thickness, camber, the
